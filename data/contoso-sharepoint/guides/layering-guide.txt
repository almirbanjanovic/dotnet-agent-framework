Layering Guide
Contoso Outdoors

The layering system is the foundation of outdoor comfort. By wearing multiple thin layers instead of one thick garment, you can regulate your temperature in changing conditions.

1. The Three-Layer System

| Layer | Purpose | Material | When to Use |
|-------|---------|----------|-------------|
| Base Layer | Moisture management — wicks sweat away from skin | Merino wool or synthetic polyester | Always. This is your foundation layer. |
| Mid Layer | Insulation — traps body heat | Fleece, down, or synthetic insulation | Cold weather, rest stops, camp |
| Outer Layer | Protection — blocks wind and rain | Waterproof-breathable shells (Gore-Tex, eVent) | Rain, wind, snow, exposed ridges |

2. Base Layer Details

The base layer is the most important layer. A wet base layer makes you cold regardless of what's on top.

- Merino wool: Naturally odor-resistant, temperature-regulating, comfortable against skin. Slightly slower drying.
- Synthetic (polyester): Dries fastest, most durable, lower cost. Can develop odor after multiple days.
- Avoid cotton: Cotton absorbs moisture, dries slowly, and causes rapid heat loss. Never wear cotton as a base layer in the backcountry.

Weight options:
- Lightweight (150g/m²): Warm weather, high-output activities
- Midweight (200g/m²): Cool weather, moderate activity (most versatile)
- Heavyweight (250g/m²): Cold weather, low activity, sleeping

3. Mid Layer Details

Choose based on temperature and activity level:

- Fleece (100-300 weight): Breathable, dries quickly, works well for active use. Not wind-resistant.
- Down jacket: Best warmth-to-weight ratio. Packable. Loses insulation when wet.
- Synthetic insulation: Nearly as warm as down, retains warmth when wet. Slightly heavier and bulkier.

4. Outer Layer Details

Your shell protects against wind and precipitation:

- Hardshell: Fully waterproof and windproof. Best for extended rain, snow, or harsh conditions.
- Softshell: Water-resistant, highly breathable. Best for active use in light precipitation or wind.
- Rain jacket: Lightweight waterproof option for warm-weather rain protection. Packs small.

5. Layering by Activity and Temperature

| Conditions | Base | Mid | Outer |
|-----------|------|-----|-------|
| Summer hiking (60-80°F) | Lightweight synthetic | None | Pack a rain shell |
| Spring/fall hiking (40-60°F) | Midweight merino | Light fleece | Softshell or rain shell |
| Cold hiking (20-40°F) | Midweight merino | Fleece + down vest | Hardshell |
| Winter / below 20°F | Heavyweight merino | Heavy fleece + down jacket | Hardshell |

6. Tips

- Ventilate before you sweat: Open zips of your outer/mid layers at the start of a climb
- Layer down at rest stops: Add your insulation layer as soon as you stop moving
- Keep your dry layers dry: Store your down jacket in a waterproof stuff sack inside your pack
