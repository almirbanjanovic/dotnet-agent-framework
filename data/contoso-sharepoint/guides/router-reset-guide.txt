How to Reset Your Router
Contoso Telecom — Customer Guide
Document ID: GUIDE-002

WHEN TO RESET YOUR ROUTER

Reset your router if you experience:
- No internet connection despite active service
- Significantly slower speeds than your subscribed tier
- Devices unable to connect to WiFi
- Router lights showing an error pattern (solid red or no lights)

SOFT RESET (RECOMMENDED FIRST STEP)

A soft reset restarts the router without erasing your settings:

1. Locate the power cable on the back of your router
2. Unplug the power cable from the router (not from the wall outlet)
3. Wait 30 seconds
4. Plug the power cable back in
5. Wait 2-3 minutes for the router to fully restart
6. All lights should return to their normal pattern (solid green or blue)
7. Try connecting to the internet again

FACTORY RESET (LAST RESORT)

A factory reset erases all custom settings (WiFi name, password, port forwarding, etc.) and returns the router to its original configuration:

1. Locate the small reset button on the back of your router (usually recessed)
2. Use a paperclip or pin to press and hold the reset button for 10 seconds
3. Release the button when the lights begin blinking rapidly
4. Wait 3-5 minutes for the router to fully restart
5. Your router will return to factory settings
6. Reconnect using the default WiFi name and password printed on the router label
7. Reconfigure any custom settings through the router admin page (192.168.1.1)

AFTER RESETTING

If the internet still does not work after a factory reset:
- Verify your service is active at myaccount.contoso.com or by calling 1-800-CONTOSO
- Check that the coaxial or fiber cable is securely connected to the router
- Try connecting a device directly to the router via Ethernet cable to rule out WiFi issues

If issues persist after these steps, contact Customer Support at 1-800-CONTOSO for further assistance. A technician visit may be required.
