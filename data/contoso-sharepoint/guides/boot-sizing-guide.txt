Boot Sizing Guide
Contoso Outdoors

Finding the right fit for hiking boots is essential for comfort and blister prevention on the trail. This guide will help you select the correct size.

1. Measuring Your Feet

- Measure your feet at the end of the day when they are slightly swollen (this mimics trail conditions)
- Stand on a piece of paper and trace the outline of your foot
- Measure from the heel to the longest toe in centimeters
- Measure both feet — use the larger measurement for sizing

3. Width Options

- Standard (D for men, B for women) — fits most feet
- Wide (EE for men, D for women) — recommended if your foot spills over the insole edges
- If between sizes, go up to the next half size for hiking boots

4. Hiking Boot Fit Tips

- Your toes should have about a thumb's width of space in front of them
- When going downhill, your toes should not touch the front of the boot
- The heel should be snug with minimal lift when walking
- Try boots on with the hiking socks you plan to wear (thicker socks require more room)
- Walk around the store or your home for at least 15 minutes before deciding

5. Break-In Period

New hiking boots require a break-in period before hitting the trail:

- Wear them around the house for 1-2 hours per day for a week
- Take short neighborhood walks (1-2 miles) before longer hikes
- Full-grain leather boots may need 2-4 weeks of break-in
- Synthetic and suede boots typically break in faster (1-2 weeks)
- If persistent hot spots develop, consider insoles or different lacing techniques

6. When to Size Up

Consider going up a half size if:
- You plan to use thick cushioned hiking socks
- You have wide feet and no wide option is available
- You hike primarily on steep downhill terrain
- You will be carrying a heavy backpack (feet swell more under load)
