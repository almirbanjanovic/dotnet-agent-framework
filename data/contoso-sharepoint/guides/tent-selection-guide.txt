Tent Selection Guide
Contoso Outdoors

Choosing the right tent depends on how you camp — car camping, backpacking, or mountaineering. This guide covers the key factors to consider.

1. Capacity

Tent capacity ratings (e.g., "4-person") refer to the maximum number of people who can lie side-by-side inside the tent. For comfort, we recommend:

- Solo camper: 2-person tent (extra room for gear)
- Couple: 3-person tent
- Family of 4: 6-person tent (room for gear and comfort)
- Backpacking: Use rated capacity minus 1 for comfortable sleeping

2. Seasonality

| Rating | Temperature Range | Best For |
|--------|------------------|----------|
| 3-Season | Spring, summer, fall (above 20°F) | Most campers. Mesh panels for ventilation, rain protection. |
| 3+ Season | Extended season (above 10°F) | Late fall and early spring. More pole structure, less mesh. |
| 4-Season | All year including winter (below 10°F) | Snow camping, mountaineering. Strongest poles, full coverage fly. |

For most recreational campers, a 3-season tent is the best choice. 4-season tents are heavier, more expensive, and can be too warm in summer.

3. Weight Considerations

| Use Case | Target Weight |
|----------|--------------|
| Car camping | No weight limit — prioritize comfort and space |
| Weekend backpacking | Under 5 lbs (2.3 kg) per person |
| Thru-hiking / ultralight | Under 2 lbs (0.9 kg) per person |
| Mountaineering | Under 4 lbs (1.8 kg) per person (durability matters more) |

4. Key Features to Consider

- Vestibules: Covered areas outside the door for storing boots and gear. Look for at least one vestibule.
- Footprint: A ground cloth that protects the tent floor. Some tents include one; others sell it separately.
- Freestanding vs. Non-freestanding: Freestanding tents can be moved after setup. Non-freestanding tents need stakes and are lighter.
- Rainfly: A full-coverage rainfly provides better weather protection than a partial fly.
- Door count: Two doors prevent climbing over your tent mate at night.
- Interior pockets: Useful for organizing headlamps, phones, and small items.

5. Setup Complexity

- Instant/pop-up: Sets up in under 2 minutes. Heavy, best for car camping.
- Hub-style: Color-coded poles click together. Setup in 5-10 minutes.
- Traditional pole-sleeve: Thread poles through sleeves. Setup in 10-15 minutes.
- Ultralight: May require trekking poles or trees for setup. Practice at home first.
