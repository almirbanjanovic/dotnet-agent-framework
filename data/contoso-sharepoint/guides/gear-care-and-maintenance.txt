Gear Care and Maintenance
Contoso Outdoors

Proper care extends the life of your outdoor gear by years. This guide covers cleaning, waterproofing, storage, and replacement guidelines for common outdoor equipment.

1. Tent Care

Cleaning:
- Shake out dirt and debris after every trip
- Spot-clean with a damp cloth and mild soap (non-detergent)
- For deep cleaning, fill a bathtub with lukewarm water and a tent-specific cleaner, soak for 20 minutes, rinse thoroughly
- Never machine wash a tent — it damages the coatings
- Never use bleach or harsh detergents

Waterproofing:
- Reapply DWR (Durable Water Repellent) spray to the rainfly every 1-2 seasons or when water stops beading on the surface
- Reapply seam sealer to all seams annually or when you notice leaks
- Apply tent floor sealant if you notice moisture seeping through

Storage:
- Always dry your tent completely before storing it — mold and mildew destroy tent fabric
- Store loosely in a large breathable cotton or mesh bag, not in the tight stuff sack
- Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight
- UV exposure degrades nylon and polyester over time

2. Backpack Care

- Empty all pockets and shake out crumbs and dirt
- Spot-clean with a damp cloth and mild soap
- For deep cleaning, hand-wash in a bathtub with lukewarm water
- Rinse thoroughly and air dry (hanging upside down)
- Never machine wash or dry — it damages the frame and coatings
- Store with all zippers open and straps loosened

3. Footwear Care

- Remove insoles and open the tongue after every hike to air out
- Brush off dried mud with a stiff brush
- Clean leather boots with a leather cleaner; clean synthetic boots with mild soap and water
- Apply leather conditioner to full-grain leather boots every 2-3 months
- Reapply DWR spray to waterproof boots when water stops beading
- Store at room temperature — avoid heat sources (radiators, car trunks in summer)
- Replace insoles every 500 miles or when they flatten out
- Replace boots when the midsole is compressed and no longer cushions, or when the outsole tread is worn smooth

4. Clothing Care

- Follow the care label — always
- Wash technical fabrics (Gore-Tex, fleece, merino) on a gentle cycle with tech-specific detergent
- Never use fabric softener on technical fabrics — it clogs the pores of waterproof membranes
- Tumble dry on low to reactivate DWR coatings (for Gore-Tex shells)
- Hang-dry down jackets, then tumble on low with clean tennis balls to restore loft

5. Stove and Cookware Care

- Let stoves cool completely before cleaning
- Wipe burner and pot supports with a damp cloth
- For stubborn residue on pots, soak in warm soapy water
- Check fuel connections and O-rings before each trip
- Store stoves with fuel disconnected

6. When to Replace Your Gear

| Item | Replace When |
|------|-------------|
| Hiking boots | Midsole compressed, outsole smooth, waterproofing fails repeatedly (typically 500-1000 miles) |
| Tent | Fabric tears easily, poles are bent or creased, persistent leaks despite resealing |
| Backpack | Frame is bent, foam padding is flat, fabric is delaminating (typically 5-10 years) |
| Rain jacket | Wetting out despite DWR reapplication, seam tape peeling (typically 3-5 years) |
| Sleeping pad | No longer holds air overnight, or foam is permanently compressed |
