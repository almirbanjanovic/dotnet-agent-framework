Backpack Fitting Guide
Contoso Outdoors

A properly fitted backpack distributes weight to your hips, not your shoulders. An ill-fitting pack causes back pain, shoulder strain, and an uncomfortable hike. This guide explains how to measure, adjust, and choose the right pack.

1. Measuring Your Torso Length

Your torso length — not your height — determines your pack size.

How to measure:
1. Tilt your head forward and find the bony bump at the base of your neck (C7 vertebra)
2. Place your hands on your hips with thumbs pointing backward along your iliac crest
3. Have someone measure the distance from C7 to an imaginary line connecting your thumbs
4. This measurement in inches is your torso length

| Torso Length | Pack Size |
|-------------|-----------|
| Under 16" | Extra Small |
| 16" - 17.5" | Small |
| 17.5" - 19.5" | Medium |
| 19.5" - 21.5" | Large |
| Over 21.5" | Extra Large |

Most packs come in S/M/L. Some have adjustable torso lengths (like our Ridgeline 65L).

2. Choosing the Right Capacity

Pack capacity is measured in liters. Choose based on trip length:

| Trip Length | Recommended Capacity |
|-------------|---------------------|
| Day hike | 15-30L |
| Overnight (1-2 nights) | 30-50L |
| Weekend (2-3 nights) | 50-65L |
| Extended trip (4-7 nights) | 65-80L |
| Thru-hike / expedition | 70L+ |

- Summit Daypack 30L — day hikes and around-town use
- Ridgeline 65L Backpack — multi-day backpacking trips

3. Hip Belt Adjustment

The hip belt should carry 80% of the pack's weight. To adjust:

1. Loosen all straps
2. Put the loaded pack on your back
3. Position the hip belt so the top of the padded section sits on your iliac crest (hip bones)
4. Tighten the hip belt snugly — it should be firm but not restrictive
5. The buckle should center on your navel

4. Shoulder Strap Adjustment

After setting the hip belt:

1. Tighten shoulder straps until they wrap over your shoulders with no gaps
2. The straps should NOT bear significant weight — they stabilize the load
3. Load lifter straps (the small straps at the top of the shoulder straps) should angle back at 25-45 degrees
4. If load lifters are horizontal or pulling too far back, the torso length is wrong

5. Sternum Strap

The sternum (chest) strap connects the shoulder straps across your chest:
- Position it about 1 inch below your collarbones
- Buckle it snugly but not tight — you should breathe freely
- It prevents the shoulder straps from sliding off your shoulders

6. Load Distribution Tips

- Pack heavy items close to your back and at mid-height (between shoulder blades and hips)
- Keep frequently used items in hip belt pockets or the top lid
- Use compression straps to cinch down a partially loaded pack
- For steep terrain, move heavy items slightly lower; for flat terrain, keep them higher
