Troubleshooting Slow Internet - Basic Steps
Contoso Telecom — Procedure Document
Effective Date: January 1, 2025
Document ID: PROC-002

PURPOSE

This procedure provides step-by-step guidance for diagnosing and resolving slow internet performance reported by customers.

INITIAL DIAGNOSIS

1. Ask the customer to run a speed test at speedtest.contoso.com and report the results.
2. Compare the measured speed against the customer's subscribed tier:
   - If speed is within 80% of the subscribed tier, performance is within acceptable range
   - If speed is below 25% of the subscribed tier, there may be a service issue

BASIC TROUBLESHOOTING

3. Verify that the router and modem are powered on and all cables are firmly connected.
4. Ask the customer to reboot the router:
   a. Unplug the power cable
   b. Wait 30 seconds
   c. Plug the power cable back in
   d. Wait 2–3 minutes for the router to fully restart
5. If the customer is on WiFi, suggest:
   a. Moving closer to the router
   b. Switching from the 2.4GHz to the 5GHz band
   c. Checking for interference from other devices (microwaves, baby monitors)

ADVANCED DIAGNOSIS

6. Check the service status dashboard for any reported outages in the customer's area.
7. Query the ServiceIncidents table or Service Monitoring System to identify any region-wide problems.
8. If a service incident is active, inform the customer and provide the estimated resolution time.

ESCALATION

9. If no service incident is found and the issue persists after basic troubleshooting:
   a. Create a support ticket with category "technical" and priority "high"
   b. Include the speed test results and troubleshooting steps already performed
   c. Escalate to the Technical Support team for deeper investigation

For questions regarding this procedure, contact Technical Support at ext. 5500.
