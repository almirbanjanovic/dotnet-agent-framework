Dropped Call Investigation Workflow
Contoso Telecom — Procedure Document
Effective Date: January 1, 2025
Document ID: PROC-005

PURPOSE

This procedure provides the workflow for investigating and resolving reported dropped call issues.

DATA COLLECTION

CSR agents should collect the following information from the customer before escalating:

1. At least 3 affected call samples, including:
   a. Date and time of each dropped call
   b. Approximate location (street intersection, building, or zip code)
   c. Called number (with customer's consent)
   d. Duration of the call before it dropped
   e. Any error messages displayed on the device

2. Device information:
   a. Make and model
   b. Operating system version
   c. Whether the device software is up-to-date

INITIAL TROUBLESHOOTING

3. Verify the customer's device software is current. If not, recommend an update.
4. Ask the customer to test calls from a different location to rule out a localized coverage gap.
5. Check if the customer is using a case or accessory that might block the antenna.

ESCALATION TO RF ENGINEERING

6. If 3 or more call samples show a pattern (same area, same time of day), escalate to the RF Engineering team.
7. Include all collected data in the escalation ticket.
8. The RF Engineering team will investigate within 5 business days and provide findings.

CUSTOMER CREDITS

If systemic network issues are identified:
- Affected customers receive account credits based on the Service Reliability SLA policy
- Credits are applied automatically once the issue is confirmed and resolved

RESOLUTION

9. Update the customer's support ticket with the investigation outcome.
10. If the issue was device-related, direct the customer to an authorized repair center.
11. If the issue was network-related, provide the estimated fix timeline and credit information.

For questions regarding this procedure, contact the RF Engineering Team at ext. 6600.
